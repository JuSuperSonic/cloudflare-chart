This Helm chart deploys the Cloudflare Tunnel (cloudflared) service into your Kubernetes cluster.

USAGE INSTRUCTIONS
-------------------
1. To verify the deployment:
   kubectl get pods --selector=app.kubernetes.io/name={{ include "cloudflare-tunnel.name" . }}

2. To check logs for the cloudflared pod:
   kubectl logs --selector=app.kubernetes.io/name={{ include "cloudflare-tunnel.name" . }}

CONFIGURATION TIPS:
-------------------
TALK ABOUT REMOTE/LOCAL
- You can configure your tunnel credentials by setting the `tunnelToken` or mounting a secret with `credentialsFile`.
- Customize the ingress rules by modifying the `ingress` section in your values.yaml.
- Set `metrics.serviceMonitor.enabled=true` to expose Prometheus metrics.
- Use `resources` to define CPU and memory limits for the cloudflared container.

POST-INSTALLATION NOTES:
-------------------------
- Ensure your DNS records in Cloudflare are correctly configured to point to the tunnel : https://dash.cloudflare.com/{{ .Values.local.accountId }}/valkhium.xyz/dns/records
- Ensure your tunnel health : https://one.dash.cloudflare.com/{{ .Values.local.accountId }}/networks/tunnels

For more information, visit the official cloudflared documentation:
https://developers.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-one/connections/connect-apps/
